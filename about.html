How it started

I was the guy Googling everything.

When I first started lifting I had no idea what I was doing. I was following random YouTube programs, eating whatever, and wondering why nothing was changing. Every program I found was either too complicated, made for someone twice my age, or just a cookie-cutter PDF that had nothing to do with my actual body or goals.

I spent more time researching than actually training. That frustrated me. There had to be a better way — something that just told me exactly what to do based on who I actually am.

The turning point

I stopped following.
I started building.

I started learning everything I could about programming, progressive overload, and aesthetic development specifically. Not just muscle — proportion. Shape. The V-taper. The kind of physique that actually looks good, not just big.

"I didn't want to just get bigger. I wanted to look like I actually trained with a purpose."

Once I understood the principles, my progress completely changed. And I realized — if I could figure this out, I could build something that handed this knowledge directly to people who are exactly where I was.

Why PHYSIQ exists

Because generic programs don't work.

Most fitness apps are built for the average person. But there is no average person. You have your own schedule, your own body, your own weak points, and your own vision for what you want to look like.

PHYSIQ exists to give everyone their own program. Not a template with your name slapped on it — an actual plan built around your inputs, your goals, and your life.

What's built

More than a program generator.

PHYSIQ now includes a full AI Physique Analyzer that scores your physique across four pillars — Development, Proportion, Conditioning, and Symmetry — with an interactive muscle map showing exactly what to work on. Upload a photo and get a breakdown in seconds.

On top of that: a Macro Calculator to dial in your nutrition, an AI Meal Planner, a Phase Nutrition planner, and a Progress Tracker that compares your photos over time. Everything in one place, all personalized to you.

The goal is to keep building — a mobile app is next. But the tools that exist right now are already more than anything I had when I started. That's the point.
