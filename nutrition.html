Beyond Macros
Micronutrients & Hormone Balance

Hitting your macros is the foundation — but the lifters who see the biggest transformations are the ones who understand what's happening beneath the surface. Micronutrients and hormones control everything from muscle synthesis to fat storage to recovery.

Key Micronutrients
☀️
Vitamin D
Directly tied to testosterone production and muscle protein synthesis. Most lifters are deficient without realizing it — especially in winter. Low Vitamin D = lower T, slower recovery, weaker immune function.
Aim: 2,000–4,000 IU/day
⚡
Zinc
Essential for testosterone synthesis and immune health. Heavy training depletes zinc through sweat. Found in red meat, oysters, and pumpkin seeds — or supplement if your diet is low in animal protein.
Aim: 15–30mg/day
😴
Magnesium
Controls sleep quality, muscle relaxation, and over 300 enzymatic reactions in the body. Poor sleep = elevated cortisol, reduced GH output, and impaired recovery. Most people don't get enough through food alone.
Aim: 300–400mg before bed
🐟
Omega-3 Fatty Acids
Reduce systemic inflammation, improve insulin sensitivity, and support joint health under heavy training load. EPA and DHA are the active forms — found in fatty fish or high-quality fish oil supplements.
Aim: 2–3g EPA/DHA daily
Hormone Balance
💪
Testosterone
The primary driver of muscle growth, fat loss, and recovery. Optimized through adequate dietary fat (don't go too low), quality sleep, heavy compound training, and managing chronic stress. Crash dieting tanks testosterone fast.
Optimize: sleep, fats, stress
😤
Cortisol
Your stress hormone — necessary in short bursts during training, destructive when chronically elevated. High cortisol breaks down muscle tissue, increases fat storage around the midsection, and impairs sleep. Manage through rest days, sleep, and avoiding extreme caloric deficits.
Manage: sleep, rest days, calories
🎯
Insulin Sensitivity
Controls how efficiently your body uses carbohydrates for muscle fuel vs fat storage. High insulin sensitivity = carbs go to muscle. Low sensitivity = carbs go to fat. Improved through consistent training, low body fat, and avoiding excessive processed sugar.
Improve: train consistently, limit sugar
🌙
Growth Hormone
Released primarily during deep sleep and peaks in the first few hours after falling asleep. GH drives fat oxidation and muscle repair overnight. Disrupted by late-night eating, alcohol, and poor sleep hygiene. Protect your sleep like it's part of your training.
Peak: deep sleep, fasted training
